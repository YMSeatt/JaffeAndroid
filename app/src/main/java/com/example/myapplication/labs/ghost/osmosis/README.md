# 🧪 Ghost Osmosis: Neural Diffusion & Knowledge Flow

Ghost Osmosis is an experimental visualization engine that models the classroom as a dynamic fluid field. It simulates how academic performance (Knowledge Potential) and behavioral patterns (Behavioral Concentration) "diffuse" between students based on their physical proximity in the seating chart.

## 🌌 The Osmotic Metaphor

In this model, every student acts as a source of "Neural Potential." When students are seated near one another, their academic and behavioral states exert "Osmotic Pressure" on the surrounding space, creating a gradient of influence that can be analyzed to identify equilibrium zones or areas of high social/academic disparity.

### 🧪 Mathematical Model: Gaussian Diffusion
The engine utilizes a **Gaussian Radial Basis Function (RBF)** to calculate the influence of a student node at any given point $(x, y)$ in the 4000x4000 logical canvas:

$$W(d) = e^{-\frac{d^2}{2\sigma^2}}$$

- **$\sigma$ (Sigma)**: Calibrated to **400 units**. This defines the "spread" of a student's influence.
- **Effective Radius**: **1000 units**. Influence beyond this distance is considered negligible for performance optimization.
- **Pre-calculated Denominator**: The constant `320,000f` ($2\sigma^2$) is used to bypass expensive square root and power operations in the high-frequency update pipeline.

## ⚡ BOLT Performance Optimizations

To maintain a fluid 60fps experience while calculating hundreds of pairwise interactions and rendering a complex fluid field:

1.  **Manual Loop Inversion**: Replaced standard Kotlin functional operators (`filter`, `map`, `average`) with manual index-based loops in `calculateStudentPotentials`. This eliminates thousands of short-lived iterator and lambda object allocations per frame.
2.  **Gaussian Optimization**: The spatial weight calculation avoids `sqrt()` by working entirely in squared-distance space until the final exponential decay.
3.  **Shader Pooling**: `GhostOsmosisLayer` maintains a `diffusionShaderPool`. This prevents the overhead of re-allocating and re-compiling AGSL `RuntimeShader` objects when the number of active diffusion patches changes.
4.  **Grid-Based Sampling**: The visual field is calculated on a 20x20 sampling grid rather than per-pixel, with the AGSL shader providing smooth interpolation and fluid noise between points.

## 🎨 Visual Encoding

The Osmosis field uses a multi-channel color mapping to represent the classroom state:

-   **Blue/Cyan (Knowledge)**: Represents high academic potential derived from Quiz and Homework logs.
-   **Green (Stability)**: Represents positive behavioral concentration.
-   **Red (Turbulence)**: Represents negative behavioral concentration.
-   **Luminosity (Pressure)**: The brightness of the fluid reflects the total "Osmotic Pressure" (the density of interactions) at that point.

## 📊 Analytical Reports

The `GhostOsmosisEngine` identifies four distinct classroom states:
-   **EQUILIBRIUM**: Knowledge and behavior are evenly distributed across the classroom.
-   **STABLE**: Nominal rates of organic knowledge exchange.
-   **HIGH_GRADIENT**: Significant disparity between adjacent students (e.g., a high-performing student seated in a cluster of low-performing students).
-   **VOID**: No student data available for analysis.
